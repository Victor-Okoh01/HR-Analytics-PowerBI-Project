# HR Analytics Dashboard | Power BI

## Improving Workforce Performance and Training ROI through Strategic HR Analytics

## Project Overview

This project was developed using Microsoft Power BI to analyse Human Resources (HR) data and provide actionable insights into employee performance, engagement, satisfaction, work-life balance, and training effectiveness.

The objective was to transform raw HR data into an interactive dashboard that enables HR managers and business leaders to make informed, data-driven decisions regarding workforce development and training investments.

The dashboard highlights trends across departments, business units, age groups, employee classifications, and training programmes to support strategic decision-making.

---

# Business Problem

The Human Resources department suspected that although significant resources were being invested in employee training, some employees were attending multiple training programmes without showing measurable improvements in performance, engagement, or satisfaction.

Additionally, management wanted to understand whether demographic factors, job classifications, or departments influenced employee performance and training outcomes.

Without a centralized reporting solution, identifying these trends was time-consuming and limited effective decision-making.

---

# Project Objectives

The project aimed to:

- Analyse employee performance across departments and business units.
- Measure employee engagement and satisfaction.
- Evaluate the effectiveness of training programmes.
- Compare internal and external training costs.
- Identify departments requiring improvement.
- Support evidence-based HR decision making.
- Improve workforce performance through data-driven insights.

---

# Business Questions

## Employee Performance & Engagement

- What is the distribution of employee satisfaction scores across departments?
- Which departments have the lowest work-life balance scores?
- Are older or younger employees reporting lower engagement scores?

## Training Effectiveness & ROI

- Which training programmes have the highest and lowest success rates?
- How does the average training cost compare between internal and external training programmes?

## Diversity & Inclusion

- Is there any noticeable performance or engagement gap across gender, race, or marital status?
- Do training outcomes vary significantly by gender, race, or age group?

## Tenure & Lifecycle Analysis

- How does employee tenure relate to performance and training outcomes?

## Cost Optimisation

- Which business units are spending the most on training without corresponding performance improvement?
- What is the average training cost per department and employee classification?

# Key Insights

The analysis identified several important findings:

- The organisation invested approximately **1.59 million** in employee training.
- The workforce consists of approximately **2,845 employees**.
- Employee engagement differs across age groups, indicating opportunities for targeted engagement strategies.
- Satisfaction scores vary between departments, suggesting that some departments require additional managerial attention.
- Work-life balance is not consistent across all departments.
- Training costs vary across business units and training types.
- Employee ratings and training outcomes can be monitored together to evaluate training effectiveness.

---

# Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are proposed:

### 1. Improve Employee Engagement

Develop targeted engagement initiatives for age groups or departments with lower engagement scores.

### 2. Optimise Training Investment

Regularly review training programmes with low completion or success rates and prioritise investment in programmes delivering measurable improvements.

### 3. Improve Work-Life Balance

Departments with lower work-life balance scores should be assessed to identify workload, scheduling, or management challenges.

### 4. Strengthen Performance Monitoring

Implement continuous monitoring of employee performance after training to evaluate long-term training effectiveness.

### 5. Support Data-Driven HR Decisions

Use the dashboard as a decision-support tool during workforce planning, budgeting, and employee development initiatives.
